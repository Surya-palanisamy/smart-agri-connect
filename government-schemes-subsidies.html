Government Schemes and Subsidies

Available Schemes

Scheme Name Description Eligibility Benefits Application Process
National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) Promotes sustainable agriculture through eco-friendly practices. Farmers with less than 2 hectares of land. Subsidy for organic inputs and water conservation techniques. Online application via the Ministry of Agriculture website.
Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY) Focuses on enhancing irrigation facilities. Farmers with existing land under cultivation. Subsidies for drip and sprinkler irrigation systems. Application through local agricultural offices.
Soil Health Management (SHM) Improves soil health through various management practices. Farmers with soil health issues or low productivity. Financial assistance for soil testing and correction measures. Apply through state agricultural departments.
Rural Development Fund Supports rural development projects including agriculture. Eligible rural areas with development projects. Grants and loans for infrastructure development. Submit proposals to the Rural Development Ministry.
Crop Insurance Scheme Provides financial support for crop loss due to unforeseen events. Farmers growing eligible crops. Compensation for crop damage due to natural calamities. Enroll through insurance providers and agricultural offices.

Application Guidelines

Scheme Name Documents Required Submission Deadline
National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) Land ownership documents, Aadhar card, Bank details. March 31st annually.
Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY) Land records, irrigation plans, Aadhar card. June 30th annually.
Soil Health Management (SHM) Soil test reports, land ownership proof. August 15th annually.
Rural Development Fund Project proposals, financial projections, implementation plans. October 31st annually.
Crop Insurance Scheme Proof of crop planting, loss assessment reports. Within 30 days of loss occurrence.
